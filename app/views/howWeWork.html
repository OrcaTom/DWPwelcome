How we work

DWP business transformation group works to 'agile' principles.

If you've never worked in an agile environment before it involves working at pace, usually releasing new features every 2 weeks.

You'll be expected to join in with agile 'ceremonies'. These include:

  • daily stand ups - 10 minutes every morning when you tell the team what you'll be working on that day
  • fortnightly 'show and tells' where you demonstrate to the team and others what you've produced in the last two weeks
  • fortnightly 'retros' where you examine with the team what went well, what went wrong and what you can do differently next time

Each of your teams will work differently and be made up of slightly different people and skills.

You'll work most closely with user researchers and interaction designers.

Agile teams sit and work together but you won't necessarily have a permanent desk. You can get a locker if you need one.

Other user experience people

You'll have regular meetings with the user experience community across DWP.

Other user experience roles include:

  • interaction designers
  • user researchers
  • content designers

Your head of role will let you know when these are happening and where.

As well as collaborating in the office individuals are expected to join the government-wide digital community, and are encouraged to blog and speak publicly about the work we are doing to improve government services.

Locations

DWP has a 'hub' strategy for delivering digital services. This means there are several large offices delivering several services from each.

Your home hub is Leeds ONE but you may need to go to the other hubs on occasion. These are:

  • Newcastle
  • Longbenton
  • Caxton House
