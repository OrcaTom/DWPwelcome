Travel and leave

You can claim expenses for travel if both:

  • you're working in any location (incuding other DWP offices) apart from your named main office
  • the travel required is not your regular commute to work

You can claim accommodation if necessary for work, but must use the approved accommodation if possible.

Time spent travelling

Time spent travelling out of hours (which includes your regular journey to work) can be claimed as flexitime.

For example if you need to catch a train at 6:30am but normally travel on the 8am train then you can claim 1 hour and 30 minutes flexitime.

Booking transport and hotels

Book all work related travel and accommodation using the trips online booking system.

Booking leave

If you want to book leave you must tell:

  • your line manager
  • the service manager of any services you're working on

Your line manager will give you a leave form on your first day to fill in.

You should ask for leave at least 2 weeks in advance of taking it.

Check with your line manager who to put in your out of office reply on your email if taking leave.
