Travel and leave

Expenses

You don't have to pay for your travel if both:

  • you're working in any location (incuding other DWP offices) apart from your named main office
  • the travel required isn't part of your regular daily travel to work

You can claim accommodation if travelling the same day is impractical.

You can claim up to £5 a day for lunch and £16 for an evening meal if away for work - make sure to keep your receipts.

Booking transport and hotels

Book all travel and accommodation using the trips online booking system if you can. It's far simpler and quicker than paying for it yourself and claiming it back as expenses.

Trips website: www.trips.uk
Your cost code (needed for booking): 173013

If you don't have a trips account the instructions for how to get one are on RM.

Time spent travelling

Time spent travelling out of hours (which includes your regular journey to work) can be claimed as flexitime.

For example if you need to catch a train at 6:30am but normally travel on the 8am train then you can claim 1 hour and 30 minutes flexitime.

Booking leave

You have an annual holiday allowance. You're also entitled to claim back any hours you've worked out of your normal hours as flexi leave.

If you want to book leave or take flexi you must tell:

  • your line manager
  • the service manager of any services you're working on

Your line manager will give you a leave form on your first day to fill in.

You should ask for leave at least 2 weeks in advance of taking it.

Check with your line manager who to put in your out of office reply on your email if taking leave.
