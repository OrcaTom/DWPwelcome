Getting started

Your first day

You should get:

  • a staff number
  • a place to sit for the first few days at least
  • any kit you need immediately to do your job, including laptop
  • a smart card to sign on to DWP desktop machines and secure laptops
  • a dwp.gsi.gov.uk email account - only accessible from DWP desktops or secure laptop
  • wifi details for your floor or team

If you don't have all of these by the end of the first day then remind your line manager for them on the morning of the second day.

You should do:

  • set up a gmail account like {yourname}.digitaldwp@gmail.com
  • join the digitalDWP slack channel, dwpdigital.slack.co.uk
  • set up the slack application on your machine
  • read the design principles
  • create a trello account if you don't already have one
  • ask for any regular meetings for your team and local user experience community and add them to your diary

Your first week

You should get:

  • a pass for the office
  • an introduction to your site
  • a timesheet to record hours worked, flexi-time and leave
  • your own laptop or desktop machine
  • an introduction to people working in similar roles
  • access to RM, the employee resource management software

If you don't have all of these by the end of the first week then ask your line manager.

You should do:

  • explore RM - this is where you submit expenses etc
  • added your bank details through RM - if you don't your salary may be delayed
  • created a tRips account to book travel and accommodation
  • joined the GOV.UK wide slack channel, you will need to use your gsi address to do this and then change it
  • emailed ed.horsford@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk from your gmail and asked to be added to the government wide design email group
  • gone to at least one meeting with the other UX people in your hub

Once you have a staff number and email you may also want to look at learning and development opportunities:

  • in the Civil Service
  • in the DWP digital academy

Questions

If you're asked to set up something you're not sure about then ask your line manager or someone in your team.
