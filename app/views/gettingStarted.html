Getting started

Your first day

You should get:

  • a staff number
  • a place to sit for the first few days at least
  • any kit you need immediately to do your job, including macbook
  • wifi details for your floor or team

If you don't have all of these by the end of the first day then remind your line manager for them on the morning of the second day.

You should:

  • set up a gmail account like {yourname}.digitaldwp@gmail.com
  • set up slack (instant messaging) on your machine
  • join the digitalDWP slack channel and introduce yourself
  • read the design principles
  • create a trello account for your digital DWP gmail - most design teams will track work using this
  • ask for any regular meetings for your team and local user experience community and add them to your diary
  • email ed.horsford@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk from your gmail and asked to be added to the digital-service-designers@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk
  • make your own copy of this DWP timesheet and add it to your google drive - update this every day

Take some time to read back through these blogs on GOV.UK:

  • Digital DWP blog
  • User research blog
  • GDS Design blog

Your first week

You should get:

  • a pass for the office
  • an orientation to your site
  • an introduction to some people working in similar roles
  • a smart card to sign on to DWP desktop machines and secure laptops
  • a dwp.gsi.gov.uk email account - only accessible from DWP desktops or secure laptop
  • access to RM, the employee resource management software

If you don't have all of these by the end of the first week then ask your line manager.

Once you have your smart card you should:

  • explore RM from a DWP laptop or desktop - this is where you submit expenses etc
  • add your bank details through RM - if you don't your salary may be delayed
  • email cd.businesstravelpolicyteam@dwp.gsi.gov.uk from your gsi email, including your staff number, and ask them to set up a www.trips.uk.com account - this is where you'll book work travel and accommodation
  • join the GOV.UK wide slack channel, you will need to use your gsi address to do this and then change it

Other things

Once you have a staff number and email you may also want to look at learning and development opportunities:

  • in the Civil Service
  • in the DWP digital academy

You may like to follow these twitter accounts for a start:

  • @BenHolliday - head of user experience
  • @kevincunnington - head of Business Transformation
  • @DigitalDWP
  • @GDSteam

Questions

If you're asked to set up something you're not sure about then ask your line manager or someone in your team.
