Getting started

Your first day

On your first day your line manager should give you:

  • a staff number
  • a place to sit for the first few days
  • any kit you need immediately to do your job
  • a smart card to sign on to DWP computers - you have to go to IT on the first floor to get this set up
  • wifi details for your floor or team

If you don't have all of these by the end of the first day then ask your line manager for them on the morning of the second day.

Your first week

In the first week you should also be given:

  • an introduction to the Leeds ONE building and your office pass - until then you'll have to sign in each morning
  • a timesheet to record hours worked, flexi-time and leave
  • your own laptop or desktop machine
  • a dwp.gsi.gov.uk email account - only accessible from DWP desktops or secure laptop
  • access to employee resource management system (RM) - only available to you with a smart card and a DWP machine

If you don't have all of these by the end of the first week then ask your line manager.

Your first month

Some things won't be urgent but eventually you'll need to set up:

  • a trips account and account to book staff travel and accommodation online - find out how on RM
  • a gmail account using google (depending on which team you're working in)
  • online accounts for collaborative tools, eg skype or Slack
  • your payment details using RM - before your first pay date

Once you have a staff number and email you may want to look at learning and development opportunities:

  • in the Civil Service
  • in the DWP digital academy

Questions

If you're asked to set up something you're not sure about then ask your line manager or someone in your team.
